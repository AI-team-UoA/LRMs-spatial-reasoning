PROMPT: If DC(x,y) and NTPPi(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given the relations:
- DC(x, y): x and y are disconnected and share no spatial parts.
- NTPPi(y, z): This is equivalent to NTPP(z, y), meaning z is a non-tangential proper part of y (z is entirely contained within y without touching its boundary).

To determine the possible relations between x and z:
- Since DC(x, y) holds, x and y have no common parts (no overlap or contact).
- NTPPi(y, z) implies z is entirely inside y (z is a proper part of y and does not touch y's boundary).

Because z is entirely contained within y and x has no spatial connection to y, x cannot share any part with z. Specifically:
- x and z cannot overlap (ruling out PO, TPP, NTPP, TPPi, NTPPi, EQ).
- x and z cannot touch (ruling out EC), as this would require a boundary connection, which would imply some spatial relation between x and y (contradicting DC(x, y)).
- The only consistent relation is DC(x, z), as x and z must be disconnected.

Thus, the only possible relation between x and z is DC.

### Answer: DC
