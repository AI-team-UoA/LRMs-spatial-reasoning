PROMPT: If TPP(x,y) and DC(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given:
- TPP(x,y): x is a tangential proper part of y (x is inside y and touches its boundary).
- DC(y,z): y and z are disconnected (no shared parts).

Analysis:
- Since x is part of y (due to TPP(x,y)), every point of x is also a point of y.
- DC(y,z) indicates that y and z have no points in common.
- Therefore, x and z cannot share any points (as x's points are part of y, which shares nothing with z).
- The only relation consistent with no shared parts is DC(x,z).

Verification of other relations:
- EC(x,z): Requires touching at a boundary, but x is entirely within y, and y is disconnected from z, so no contact is possible.
- PO(x,z): Requires overlapping interiors, impossible due to DC(y,z).
- TPP(x,z), NTPP(x,z): Require x to be inside z, but z is disconnected from y (which contains x), impossible.
- TPPi(x,z), NTPPi(x,z): Require z to be inside x, but x is inside y and y is disconnected from z, impossible.
- EQ(x,z): Requires x and z to be identical, but then y and z would share parts (via x), contradicting DC(y,z).

Conclusion:
The only possible relation between x and z is DC(x,z).

### Answer: DC(x,z)
