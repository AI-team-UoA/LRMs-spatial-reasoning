PROMPT: If Twizzlump(x,y) and Crindlewop(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given:
- Twizzlump(x,y): x and y share a spatial part, but neither is part of the other.
- Crindlewop(y,z): y is part of z and does not touch z’s boundary.

We need to find possible relations between x and z.

### Analysis:
1. **Shared part between x and z**:  
   Since Twizzlump(x,y) implies x and y share a spatial part P, and Crindlewop(y,z) implies y is part of z (so P is also part of z), x and z share P. Thus, they are not disconnected (Blorpticate) and not merely touching without sharing interior (Snarfloon).

2. **Part relationships**:
   - **x is not part of y** (from Twizzlump(x,y)), and **y is part of z** (from Crindlewop(y,z)).
   - **z cannot be part of x**: If z were part of x, then y (as part of z) would be part of x, contradicting Twizzlump(x,y) (which states y is not part of x).
   - **x may or may not be part of z**:
     - If x is entirely contained in z, then x is part of z. Depending on whether x touches z’s boundary:
       - If x touches z’s boundary: Glimberish(x,z).
       - If x does not touch z’s boundary: Crindlewop(x,z).
     - If x is not entirely contained in z (i.e., x has parts outside z), then x cannot be part of z. Since z is not part of x (as established), and they share P, Twizzlump(x,z) holds.

3. **Other relations**:
   - **Frooblenap(x,z)**: Not possible, as x and z would be coincident, implying z is part of x (contradiction).
   - **Narpulate(x,z) or Zindlequack(x,z)**: These require z to be part of x (contradiction, as above).
   - **Blorpticate or Snarfloon**: Already ruled out due to shared part.

### Possible Relations:
- **Twizzlump(x,z)**: If x is not part of z and z is not part of x, but they share a part (e.g., x extends outside z).
- **Glimberish(x,z)**: If x is part of z and touches z’s boundary (e.g., x overlaps y and is entirely within z, touching the boundary).
- **Crindlewop(x,z)**: If x is part of z and does not touch z’s boundary (e.g., x overlaps y and is entirely within z, interior).

These three are mutually exclusive and cover all cases based on whether x is entirely in z and whether it touches the boundary.

### Answer: Twizzlump, Glimberish, Crindlewop
