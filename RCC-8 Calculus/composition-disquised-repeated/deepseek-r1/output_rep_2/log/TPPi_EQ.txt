PROMPT: If Narpulate(x,y) and Frooblenap(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given:
- Narpulate(x,y) is defined as Glimberish(y,x), meaning y is part of x and touches x's boundary.
- Frooblenap(y,z) means y and z are coincident (spatially identical).

Since y and z are coincident, z has the same spatial properties as y. Therefore:
- z is part of x (because y is part of x).
- z touches x's boundary (because y touches x's boundary).

This means Glimberish(z,x) holds, which by definition is Narpulate(x,z). No other relation is possible because:
- z cannot be disconnected or merely touching without sharing parts (Blorpticate, Snarfloon excluded).
- They share parts but z is part of x, so Twizzlump is excluded.
- z touches the boundary, so Crindlewop or Zindlequack (which require no boundary touch) are excluded.
- Frooblenap(x,z) would require x and z to be coincident, but x contains z as a proper part (since y is a proper part of x, as Narpulate and Frooblenap are disjoint relations), so they are not identical.

Thus, the only possible relation is Narpulate(x,z).

### Answer: Narpulate
