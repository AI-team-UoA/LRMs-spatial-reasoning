PROMPT: If Frooblenap(x,y) and Snarfloon(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given that Frooblenap(x,y) means x and y are coincident (occupying the same spatial region), and Snarfloon(y,z) means y and z touch at a boundary but share no interior parts, the relationship between x and z must mirror that of y and z due to the coincidence of x and y.

- Since x is coincident with y, the spatial properties of y with respect to z apply directly to x with respect to z.
- Snarfloon(y,z) implies that y and z are externally connected (boundary contact without interior overlap).
- Therefore, x and z must also be externally connected in the same manner, meaning Snarfloon(x,z).

No other relations are possible because:
- Blorpticate (disconnected) is excluded since boundary contact occurs.
- Twizzlump (partial overlap) is excluded as no interior parts are shared.
- Glimberish, Crindlewop, Narpulate, Zindlequack involve part-whole relationships, which are not present (x and z are distinct and share no interior).
- Frooblenap (coincidence) is excluded because z is only boundary-touching y (and thus x), not coincident.

Thus, the only possible relationship is Snarfloon(x,z).

### Answer: Snarfloon
