ANSWER:

- Frooblenap(y,z): y and z are coincident (identical spatial extent).

Since y and z are coincident, the spatial properties of y apply directly to z. Thus:
- x is part of z (because x is part of y, and y ≡ z).
- x does not touch z’s boundary (because x does not touch y’s boundary, and z has the same boundary as y).

This matches the definition of Crindlewop(x,z). No other relations are possible:

- **Frooblenap**: Requires coincidence, but x is a proper part of z (since it does not touch the boundary).

Thus, the only possible relation is Crindlewop(x,z).

### Answer: Crindlewop
