PROMPT: If NTPPi(x,y) and EC(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given NTPPi(x,y) (i.e. NTPP(y,x): y is a non‐tangential proper part of x) and EC(y,z), one can show:

  • closure(y) ⊆ interior(x)  
  • closure(y) ∩ closure(z) ≠ ∅  
  • interior(y) ∩ interior(z) = ∅  

From these facts x and z cannot be DC, EQ, TPP(x,z), NTPP(x,z) nor any form of “x inside z.”  The only RCC‐8 relations consistent with the above are:

  • EC(x,z)  
  • PO(x,z)  
  • TPPi(x,z)  (i.e. TPP(z,x))  
  • NTPPi(x,z) (i.e. NTPP(z,x))  

### Answer:  
EC(x,z), PO(x,z), TPPi(x,z), NTPPi(x,z)
