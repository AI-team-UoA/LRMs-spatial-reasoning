PROMPT: If DC(x,y) and TPPi(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given DC(x,y) and TPPi(y,z), the possible relationship between x and z is DC(x,z). This is because:

- DC(x,y) means x and y are disconnected and share no spatial parts.
- TPPi(y,z) is equivalent to TPP(z,y), meaning z is a tangential proper part of y (i.e., z is part of y and touches y's boundary).
- Since z is entirely within y (as a proper part), and x is disconnected from y, x must also be disconnected from z. Any overlap or contact between x and z would imply a connection between x and y, contradicting DC(x,y).

Thus, DC(x,z) is the only possible relation.
