PROMPT: If Glimberish(x,y) and Crindlewop(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given Glimberish(x,y) and Crindlewop(y,z), the possible relationship between x and z is Crindlewop(x,z).

- Glimberish(x,y) means x is a proper part of y and touches y's boundary.
- Crindlewop(y,z) means y is a proper part of z and does not touch z's boundary.
- Since x is part of y and y is part of z, x must be part of z by transitivity.
- Because y does not touch z's boundary and x is part of y, x cannot touch z's boundary (as all points of x are interior to z).
- Therefore, x is part of z and does not touch z's boundary, which corresponds to Crindlewop(x,z).

### Answer:
