PROMPT: If Narpulate(x,y) and Frooblenap(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Since y and z are coincident, the spatial relationship between x and z must mirror that between x and y. Specifically:
- y is part of x → z is part of x (because z is coincident with y).
- y touches x's boundary → z touches x's boundary (because z is coincident with y).

This satisfies the definition of Glimberish(z,x), which is equivalent to Narpulate(x,z). No other relation from the set is possible because:

- Crindlewop(x,z) requires z to be part of x without touching the boundary, but z touches the boundary.

- Frooblenap(x,z) requires coincidence, but z is a proper part of x (since Narpulate(x,y) implies y, and thus z, is not coincident with x by mutual exclusivity of the relations).

Thus, the only possible relation is Narpulate(x,z).

### Answer: Narpulate(x,z)
