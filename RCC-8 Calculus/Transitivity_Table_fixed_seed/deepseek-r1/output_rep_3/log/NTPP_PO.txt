PROMPT: If NTPP(x,y) and PO(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given NTPP(x, y) and PO(y, z), the possible relationships between x and z are determined by the spatial configurations. NTPP(x, y) means x is a non-tangential proper part of y (entirely inside y without touching its boundary). PO(y, z) means y and z partially overlap (share some interior parts but neither is part of the other).

The possible relations between x and z are:
- **DC(x, z)**: Disconnected. This occurs if x is entirely within the non-overlapping part of y (relative to z), so x and z share no points.
- **EC(x, z)**: Externally connected. This occurs if x is in the non-overlapping part of y but touches the boundary of z at some point.
- **PO(x, z)**: Partially overlapping. This occurs if x spans both the overlapping and non-overlapping parts of y (relative to z), so x and z share some interior parts but neither is part of the other.
- **TPP(x, z)**: Tangential proper part. This occurs if x is entirely within the overlapping part of y and z and touches the boundary of z (but not the boundary of y, satisfying NTPP(x, y)).
- **NTPP(x, z)**: Non-tangential proper part. This occurs if x is entirely within the overlapping part of y and z and does not touch the boundary of z.

These five relations are possible, while EQ, TPPi, and NTPPi are not possible due to the constraints of NTPP(x, y) and PO(y, z).

### Answer: DC, EC, PO, TPP, NTPP
