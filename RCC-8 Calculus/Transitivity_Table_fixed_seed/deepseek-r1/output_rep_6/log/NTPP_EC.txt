PROMPT: If NTPP(x,y) and EC(y,z) then what are the possible relationships between x and z?

ANSWER:

Given NTPP(x,y) and EC(y,z), the possible relationship between x and z is DC(x,z), meaning x and z are disconnected and share no spatial parts.

- NTPP(x,y) indicates that x is entirely within the interior of y and does not touch y's boundary.
- EC(y,z) indicates that y and z touch at a boundary but share no interior parts.
- Since x is strictly inside y and not on the boundary, and y only interacts with z at its boundary, x and z have no points in common. Therefore, x and z must be disconnected (DC).

No other RCC-8 relations are possible, as:
- EC(x,z) is impossible because x cannot touch z directly due to the gap from x not touching y's boundary.
- PO(x,z) is impossible because there is no overlap in interiors or boundaries.
- TPP(x,z), NTPP(x,z), TPPi(x,z), NTPPi(x,z), and EQ(x,z) are impossible because x is not part of z, z is not part of x, and they are not coincident.

### Answer: DC
