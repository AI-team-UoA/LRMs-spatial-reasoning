PROMPT: Let  Southwest and  West be cardinal direction relations. If region x is  Southwest of region y and region y is  West of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

Let x, y, and z be regions. The relations are given as:
1. Region x is Southwest of region y (x SW y).
2. Region y is West of region z (y W z).

Let's analyze these relations using a coordinate system, placing the reference region's center or significant point at the origin.

Statement 2: y is West of z.
If z is at (0,0), region y is located generally to the left of z. In terms of coordinates, if y's position is (y_x, y_y) relative to z, this implies y_x < 0. The y-coordinate, y_y, can be positive (y is Northwest of z), zero (y is strictly West of z), or negative (y is Southwest of z), depending on the definition of "West" (e.g., a broad sector, a half-plane x < 0, or a narrow band around the negative x-axis). For this analysis, we will consider the general case where y_x < 0.

Statement 1: x is Southwest of y.
If y is at (y_x, y_y), region x is located generally to the southwest of y. If x's position is (x_x, x_y) relative to y, this implies x_x < 0 and x_y < 0.
So, the coordinates of x relative to z are the sum of the coordinates of y relative to z and the coordinates of x relative to y.
Let the position of z be (0,0).
Let the position of y relative to z be (y_x, y_y), with y_x < 0.
Let the position of x relative to y be (v_x, v_y), with v_x < 0 and v_y < 0 (representing the Southwest vector from y to x).

The position of x relative to z is (z_x, z_y) where:
z_x = y_x + v_x
z_y = y_y + v_y

Since y_x < 0 and v_x < 0, their sum z_x = y_x + v_x is always negative.
This means that region x is always located to the West of the vertical line passing through region z.
This rules out the relations East, Northeast, and Southeast between x and z. North and South are also ruled out as z_x cannot be zero.

Now let's look at the y-coordinate of x relative to z: z_y = y_y + v_y.
We know v_y < 0. The sign of z_y depends on the value of y_y.

Case 1: y is in the Northwest part of the "West of z" region.
y_x < 0 and y_y > 0.
z_x = y_x + v_x < 0.
z_y = y_y + v_y. Since y_y > 0 and v_y < 0, z_y could be positive, zero, or negative.
- If y_y > |v_y|, then z_y > 0. (z_x < 0, z_y > 0) -> x is Northwest of z.
  Example: z=(0,0), y=(-5, 3). v=(-1, -1). x = (-5-1, 3-1) = (-6, 2). (-6, 2) is Northwest of (0,0).
- If y_y = |v_y|, then z_y = 0. (z_x < 0, z_y = 0) -> x is West of z.
  Example: z=(0,0), y=(-5, 1). v=(-1, -1). x = (-5-1, 1-1) = (-6, 0). (-6, 0) is West of (0,0).
- If y_y < |v_y|, then z_y < 0. (z_x < 0, z_y < 0) -> x is Southwest of z.
  Example: z=(0,0), y=(-5, 1). v=(-1, -3). x = (-5-1, 1-3) = (-6, -2). (-6, -2) is Southwest of (0,0).
Thus, Northwest, West, and Southwest are all possible when y is in the Northwest part of the West region relative to z.

Case 2: y is strictly West of z.
y_x < 0 and y_y = 0.
z_x = y_x + v_x < 0.
z_y = y_y + v_y = 0 + v_y = v_y < 0.
(z_x < 0, z_y < 0) -> x is always Southwest of z.
Example: z=(0,0), y=(-5, 0). v=(-1, -1). x = (-5-1, 0-1) = (-6, -1). (-6, -1) is Southwest of (0,0).

Case 3: y is in the Southwest part of the "West of z" region.
y_x < 0 and y_y < 0.
z_x = y_x + v_x < 0.
z_y = y_y + v_y. Since y_y < 0 and v_y < 0, their sum z_y = y_y + v_y is always negative.
(z_x < 0, z_y < 0) -> x is always Southwest of z.
Example: z=(0,0), y=(-5, -3). v=(-1, -1). x = (-5-1, -3-1) = (-6, -4). (-6, -4) is Southwest of (0,0).

Based on point or centroid analysis, the possible cardinal direction relations are Northwest, West, and Southwest.

The option "Bounding Box" is often used to describe relations between regions when they overlap in a way that is not captured by a single cardinal direction (e.g., overlapping bounding boxes in one dimension while disjoint in another) or when the regions themselves overlap.
If the cardinal relations imply disjointness of bounding boxes (BB), then:
BB(y) is West of BB(z) => BB(y).max_x < BB(z).min_x.
BB(x) is SW of BB(y) => BB(x).max_x < BB(y).min_x AND BB(x).max_y < BB(y).min_y.
From these, BB(x).max_x < BB(y).max_x < BB(z).min_x. So BB(x) is always West of BB(z).
The y-relation between BB(x) and BB(z) depends on BB(x).max_y compared to BB(z)'s y-range [BB(z).min_y, BB(z).max_y].
BB(x).max_y < BB(y).min_y. BB(y).min_y can be anywhere relative to BB(z)'s y-range.
If BB(y).min_y is high enough (y is Northwest of z in BB sense), BB(x).max_y could be above, within, or below BB(z)'s y-range, leading to Northwest, Bounding Box (y-overlap), or Southwest relations between x and z respectively.
Example for Bounding Box:

y = [-5,-2]x[0.5, 3]. BB(y).max_x=-2<0. BB(y).min_y=0.5. (y is West of z, y-overlaps/is North of z).

Let x = [-7,-6]x[0.1, 0.4]. BB(x).max_x = -6 < -5. BB(x).max_y = 0.4 < 0.5. (x is SW of y).
Compare BB(x) with BB(z): BB(x)=[-7,-6]x[0.1, 0.4], BB(z)=[0,1]x[0,1].
BB(x).max_x = -6 < BB(z).min_x=0 (West).
BB(x).min_y=0.1, BB(x).max_y=0.4. BB(z).min_y=0, BB(z).max_y=1.
BB(x).min_y > BB(z).min_y and BB(x).max_y < BB(z).max_y. BB(x) is within the y-range of BB(z).
This scenario, where x is strictly West of z but vertically overlapping, does not fit a simple cardinal direction like Northwest, West, or Southwest if these imply disjointness or specific alignment. Therefore, "Bounding Box" is a possible relation to describe this configuration.

All the relations Northwest, West, Southwest have been shown to be possible. The "Bounding Box" relation is also possible under interpretations allowing for overlap in one axis when disjoint in the other, or for general complex spatial relations not fitting a single direction.
