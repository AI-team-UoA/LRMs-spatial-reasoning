ANSWER:

Based on the given cardinal direction relations:
- Region \(x\) is Southwest of region \(y\).
- Region \(y\) is Southwest of region \(z\).

The Southwest relation implies that \(x\) is entirely to the south and west of \(y\), and \(y\) is entirely to the south and west of \(z\). By transitivity:
- \(x\) must be entirely to the south of \(z\) (since \(x\) is south of \(y\) and \(y\) is south of \(z\)).
- \(x\) must be entirely to the west of \(z\) (since \(x\) is west of \(y\) and \(y\) is west of \(z\)).

Therefore, \(x\) is both south and west of \(z\), which corresponds to the **Southwest** relation. No other cardinal direction relation (e.g., South, West, Southeast, etc.) or the Bounding Box relation is possible under this transitivity, as the conditions strictly satisfy Southwest.

Thus, the only possible relation between region \(x\) and region \(z\) is:
**Southwest**
