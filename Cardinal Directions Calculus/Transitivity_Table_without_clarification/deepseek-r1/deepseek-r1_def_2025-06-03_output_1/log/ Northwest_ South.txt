PROMPT: Let  Northwest and  South be cardinal direction relations. If region x is  Northwest of region y and region y is  South of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

- Region \(x\) is **Northwest** of region \(y\), meaning \(x\) is to the northwest of \(y\) (i.e., west and north of \(y\)).

   - \(x\) is northwest of \(y\), so \(x\) is west of \(y\).
   - Therefore, \(x\) must be west of \(z\) (since \(y\) and \(z\) are aligned).

2. **Vertical Component**:
   - \(y\) is south of \(z\), so \(y\) is below \(z\).
   - \(x\) is northwest of \(y\), so \(x\) is above \(y\).
   - The vertical position of \(x\) relative to \(z\) depends on the distances:
     - If the northward distance from \(y\) to \(x\) is **greater** than the southward distance from \(z\) to \(y\), then \(x\) ends up above \(z\) (north component).
     - If it is **less**, \(x\) ends up below \(z\) (south component).
     - If it is **equal**, \(x\) is at the same vertical level as \(z\) (aligned vertically).

Combining the horizontal and vertical components:
- **West** if \(x\) is vertically aligned with \(z\).
- **Northwest** if \(x\) is above \(z\).
- **Southwest** if \(x\) is below \(z\).

Thus, the possible relations between \(x\) and \(z\) are **Northwest**, **West**, and **Southwest**. No other relations are possible given the constraints.

**Answer: Northwest, West, Southwest**
