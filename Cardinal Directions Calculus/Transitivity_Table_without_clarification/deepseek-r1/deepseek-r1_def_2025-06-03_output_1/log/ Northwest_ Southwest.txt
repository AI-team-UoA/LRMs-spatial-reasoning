ANSWER:

- Region x is Northwest of region y: x is north and west of y.
- Region y is Southwest of region z: y is south and west of z.

   - x is west of y (from x Northwest of y).
   - y is west of z (from y Southwest of z).
   - Therefore, x is west of z (transitivity in the west direction).

2. **North-South Component**:
   - x is north of y (from x Northwest of y).
   - y is south of z (from y Southwest of z).
   - The north-south relation between x and z depends on the specific coordinates:
     - If x is north of z (y-coordinate of x > y-coordinate of z), then x is **Northwest** of z (west and north).
     - If x is south of z (y-coordinate of x < y-coordinate of z), then x is **Southwest** of z (west and south).
     - If x and z share the same latitude (y-coordinate of x = y-coordinate of z), then x is **West** of z (only west, no north/south component).

Given the constraints, the possible relations between x and z are:
- **Northwest** (if x is north of z),
- **Southwest** (if x is south of z),
- **West** (if x and z are at the same latitude).

No other cardinal directions (e.g., North, South, East, Northeast, Southeast, Northwest, or Bounding Box) are possible due to the definite west component and variable north-south alignment.

**Answer: Northwest, Southwest, West**
