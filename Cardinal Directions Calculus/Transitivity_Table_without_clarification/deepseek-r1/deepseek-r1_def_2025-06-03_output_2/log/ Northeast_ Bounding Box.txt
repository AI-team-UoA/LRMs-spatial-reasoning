ANSWER:

Given that region \(x\) is **Northeast** of region \(y\) and region \(y\) is the **Bounding Box** of region \(z\), the possible relation between \(x\) and \(z\) is analyzed as follows:

- **Northeast** means \(x\) is entirely to the north and east of \(y\). Specifically, the minimum \(x\)-coordinate of \(x\) is greater than the maximum \(x\)-coordinate of \(y\), and the minimum \(y\)-coordinate of \(x\) is greater than the maximum \(y\)-coordinate of \(y\).
- **Bounding Box** means \(y\) is the minimal axis-aligned rectangle enclosing \(z\). Thus, \(z\) is entirely within \(y\), so the maximum \(x\)-coordinate of \(z\) is less than or equal to the maximum \(x\)-coordinate of \(y\), and the maximum \(y\)-coordinate of \(z\) is less than or equal to the maximum \(y\)-coordinate of \(y\).

Combining these:
- Since \(x\) is northeast of \(y\), and \(z\) is inside \(y\), \(x\) must be entirely to the east of \(z\) (because the minimum \(x\)-coordinate of \(x\) exceeds the maximum \(x\)-coordinate of \(y\), which is greater than or equal to the maximum \(x\)-coordinate of \(z\)).
- Similarly, \(x\) must be entirely to the north of \(z\) (because the minimum \(y\)-coordinate of \(x\) exceeds the maximum \(y\)-coordinate of \(y\), which is greater than or equal to the maximum \(y\)-coordinate of \(z\)).

Therefore, \(x\) is both north and east of \(z\), satisfying the definition of **Northeast**. No other cardinal direction relation (e.g., North, East, etc.) is possible because \(x\) must be strictly northeast relative to \(z\)'s position within \(y\).

**Possible relation between \(x\) and \(z\): Northeast**
