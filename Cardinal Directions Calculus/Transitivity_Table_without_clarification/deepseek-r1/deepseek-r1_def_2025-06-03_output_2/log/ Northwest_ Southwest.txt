- Region \(x\) is **Northwest** of region \(y\).
- Region \(y\) is **Southwest** of region \(z\).

- From \(z\) to \(y\): Southwest (SW) direction, which has components west and south.
- From \(y\) to \(x\): Northwest (NW) direction, which has components west and north.

The combined direction from \(z\) to \(x\) is influenced by the magnitudes:
- If the northward component (from \(y\) to \(x\)) dominates, \(x\) is **Northwest** of \(z\).
- If the southward component (from \(z\) to \(y\)) dominates, \(x\) is **Southwest** of \(z\).
- If the westward components balance such that the north/south components cancel, \(x\) is **West** of \(z\).

Thus, the possible relations between \(x\) and \(z\) are:
- **West**
- **Northwest**
- **Southwest**
