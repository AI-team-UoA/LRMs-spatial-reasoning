- **Southeast (SE)** of y means x is south and east of y.
- **Northwest (NW)** of z means y is north and west of z.

The net displacement from z to x depends on the magnitudes of these movements:
- The east-west component: x could be east, west, or directly aligned with z.
- The north-south component: x could be north, south, or directly aligned with z.

All nine cardinal direction relations (including Bounding Box for exact alignment) are possible:
- **Northeast**: If x is east and north of z.
- **East**: If x is directly east of z (same latitude, east longitude).

- **South**: If x is directly south of z (same longitude, south latitude).
- **Southwest**: If x is west and south of z.
- **West**: If x is directly west of z (same latitude, west longitude).
- **Northwest**: If x is west and north of z.
- **North**: If x is directly north of z (same longitude, north latitude).
- **Bounding Box**: If x and z coincide or share boundaries (exact position alignment).

Thus, the possible relations between x and z are:  
**North, Northeast, East, Southeast, South, Southwest, West, Northwest, Bounding Box**.
