ANSWER:

Based on the cardinal direction relations given:

- Region \(x\) is **South** of region \(y\), meaning \(x\) is located southward relative to \(y\).
- Region \(y\) is **Northeast** of region \(z\), meaning \(y\) is northeast relative to \(z\).

To determine the possible relations between \(x\) and \(z\), consider the composition of these directions. Moving from \(z\) to \(y\) in the **Northeast** direction and then from \(y\) to \(x\) in the **South** direction results in a net displacement that can fall into one of several directional cones relative to \(z\):

1. **East**: If the southward movement from \(y\) to \(x\) has minimal or no east-west deviation, and the net displacement places \(x\) directly east of \(z\).  
   Example: \(z\) at \((0,0)\), \(y\) at \((10,10)\), \(x\) at \((10,0)\) → vector from \(z\) to \(x\) is \((10,0)\), which is due east.

2. **Southeast**: If the southward movement from \(y\) to \(x\) includes an eastward component or the net displacement angles southeast.  
   Example: \(z\) at \((0,0)\), \(y\) at \((10,10)\), \(x\) at \((10,-5)\) → vector from \(z\) to \(x\) is \((10,-5)\), which is southeast.

3. **South**: If the southward movement from \(y\) to \(x\) is significant and the net displacement aligns southward relative to \(z\).

These possibilities arise because:
- The **Northeast** movement from \(z\) to \(y\) adds positive east and north components.
- The **South** movement from \(y\) to \(x\) adds a south component (negative north) with possible east-west variation within the south cone.
- The net effect can place \(x\) in the **East**, **Southeast**, or **South** direction relative to \(z\).

Thus, the possible relations between \(x\) and \(z\) are **East**, **Southeast**, and **South**.
