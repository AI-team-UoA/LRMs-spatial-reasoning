- Since \(y\) is southeast of \(z\), \(z\) must be northwest of \(y\).
- \(x\) is the bounding box of \(y\), meaning \(x\) has the same minimum and maximum coordinates as \(y\) in both the east-west and north-south directions.
- Therefore, relative to \(z\), \(x\) is also southeast of \(z\) because:
  - \(x\) is at least as far east as \(y\) (since they share the same eastern boundary), satisfying the "east" component.
  - \(x\) is at least as far south as \(y\) (since they share the same southern boundary), satisfying the "south" component.
- Thus, \(x\) is southeast of \(z\).

No other cardinal direction relations (e.g., north, northeast, south, east, northwest, west, southwest) or the bounding box relation are possible, as \(z\) cannot be inside \(x\) (since \(z\) is northwest of \(y\) and \(x\) only encloses \(y\)) and the directional constraints are fixed.

The possible relation between region \(x\) and region \(z\) is **Southeast**.
