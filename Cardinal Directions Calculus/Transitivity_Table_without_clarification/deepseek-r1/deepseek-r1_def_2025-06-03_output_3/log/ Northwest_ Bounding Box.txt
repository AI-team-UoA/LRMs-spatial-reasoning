ANSWER:

Based on the given relations:

- Region \(x\) is **Northwest** of region \(y\). This means \(x\) is entirely north and entirely west of \(y\). Specifically:
  - The entire \(x\) is north of the northernmost point of \(y\).
  - The entire \(x\) is west of the westernmost point of \(y\).
- Region \(y\) is the **Bounding Box** of region \(z\). This means \(y\) is the minimal axis-aligned rectangle containing \(z\), so \(z\) is entirely within \(y\). Specifically:
  - The northernmost point of \(z\) is at most as north as the northernmost point of \(y\).
  - The southernmost point of \(z\) is at least as south as the southernmost point of \(y\).
  - The easternmost point of \(z\) is at most as east as the easternmost point of \(y\).
  - The westernmost point of \(z\) is at least as west as the westernmost point of \(y\).

Now, consider the relation between \(x\) and \(z\):

- **North-south direction**: Since \(x\) is entirely north of \(y\) (i.e., the southernmost point of \(x\) is north of the northernmost point of \(y\)), and \(z\) is entirely within \(y\) (i.e., the northernmost point of \(z\) is at most as north as the northernmost point of \(y\)), it follows that the entire \(x\) is north of the entire \(z\). Specifically, the southernmost point of \(x\) is north of the northernmost point of \(z\).
- **East-west direction**: Since \(x\) is entirely west of \(y\) (i.e., the easternmost point of \(x\) is west of the westernmost point of \(y\)), and \(z\) is entirely within \(y\) (i.e., the westernmost point of \(z\) is at least as west as the westernmost point of \(y\)), it follows that the entire \(x\) is west of the entire \(z\). Specifically, the easternmost point of \(x\) is west of the westernmost point of \(z\).

Thus, \(x\) is entirely north and entirely west of \(z\), satisfying the definition of **Northwest**. This holds regardless of the specific position of \(z\) within \(y\) (e.g., even if \(z\) is in the southeast corner of \(y\), the constraints ensure \(x\) remains northwest of \(z\)).

No other cardinal direction relation is possible because:
- \(x\) cannot be south or east of \(z\) due to the north and west constraints.
- Mixed relations (e.g., Northeast, Southwest) are excluded because \(x\) is strictly north and west of \(z\).
- Direct cardinal directions (e.g., North, West) alone are insufficient as both conditions must hold simultaneously.

Therefore, the only possible relation between \(x\) and \(z\) is **Northwest**.

**Answer: Northwest**
