- **Northwest**: \(x\) is entirely above and entirely west of \(z\).  
  Example: \(x\) is higher and to the left of \(z\).
- **Bounding Box**: \(x\) and \(z\) overlap in both horizontal and vertical dimensions, so no strict cardinal direction applies.  
  Example: \(x\) and \(z\) partially overlap in space.

Thus, every relation is possible depending on the specific placement of \(x\) and \(z\) relative to each other, while satisfying the initial conditions.
