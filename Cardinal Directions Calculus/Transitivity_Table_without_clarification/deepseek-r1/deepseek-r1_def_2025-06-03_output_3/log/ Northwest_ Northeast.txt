ANSWER:

Based on the given relations that region \(x\) is Northwest of region \(y\) and region \(y\) is Northeast of region \(z\), the possible cardinal direction relations between region \(x\) and region \(z\) are derived as follows:

- \(x\) is always north of \(z\) because \(y\) is north of \(z\) (from \(y\) being Northeast of \(z\)) and \(x\) is north of \(y\) (from \(x\) being Northwest of \(y\)).
- The east-west position of \(x\) relative to \(z\) depends on the relative movements:
  - If the westward move from \(y\) to \(x\) is less than the eastward move from \(z\) to \(y\), \(x\) may be east of \(z\), leading to **East** or **Northeast**.
  - If the westward move from \(y\) to \(x\) equals the eastward move from \(z\) to \(y\), \(x\) is directly **North**.
  - If the westward move from \(y\) to \(x\) is greater than the eastward move from \(z\) to \(y\), \(x\) is west of \(z\), leading to **Northwest**.
