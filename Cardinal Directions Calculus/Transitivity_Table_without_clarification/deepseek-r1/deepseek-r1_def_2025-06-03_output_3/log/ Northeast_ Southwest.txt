- x could be North of z (if x is further North than z).
- x could be South of z (if x is less North than z).
- x could be East of z (if x is further East than z).
- x could be West of z (if x is less East than z).

Considering all combinations of these, the possible cardinal direction relations between x and z are all eight directions:
- **North**: x is directly North of z (same East, x more North).
- **Northeast**: x is North and East of z.
- **East**: x is directly East of z (same North, x more East).
- **Southeast**: x is South and East of z.
- **South**: x is directly South of z (same East, x less North).
- **Southwest**: x is South and West of z.
- **West**: x is directly West of z (same North, x less East).
- **Northwest**: x is North and West of z.

These are all possible because the exact positions relative to y do not constrain the relative positions between x and z beyond both being Northeast of y. Examples can be constructed for each case, as shown in the reasoning.

**North, Northeast, Southeast, South, East, Northwest, West, Southwest**
