PROMPT: Let E and E be cardinal direction relations. If region x is E of region y and region y is E of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

From the defining conditions,  
•  x E y means  
   (1) supx(y) ≤ infx(x),  
   (2) infy(y) ≤ infy(x), and  
   (3) supy(x) ≤ supy(y).  
•  y E z means  
   (4) supx(z) ≤ infx(y),  
   (5) infy(z) ≤ infy(y), and  
   (6) supy(y) ≤ supy(z).  

We want the possible relation(s) between x and z.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. X‐coordinate chaining
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

From (1) and (4):
•  supx(y) ≤ infx(x)        [x is to the right of y]
•  supx(z) ≤ infx(y)        [y is to the right of z]

By transitivity these imply

   supx(z) ≤ infx(y) ≤ supx(y) ≤ infx(x).

Hence, in the x‐direction, z is leftmost, then y, then x is rightmost (possibly touching at the boundaries).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. Y‐coordinate containment
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

From (2)–(3) and (5)–(6):
•  infy(y) ≤ infy(x)  and  supy(x) ≤ supy(y)     [x’s y‐extent is within y’s]
•  infy(z) ≤ infy(y)  and  supy(y) ≤ supy(z)     [y’s y‐extent is within z’s]

Transitivity then gives

   infy(z) ≤ infy(x)   and   supy(x) ≤ supy(z),

so x’s bounding box is also “vertically contained” in z’s bounding box.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. Checking x E z
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The definition of x E z requires:  
•  supx(z) ≤ infx(x),  
•  infy(z) ≤ infy(x),  
•  supy(x) ≤ supy(z).  

We already found exactly these inequalities above:
•  supx(z) ≤ infx(x)  (from the chain in the x‐coordinates),  
•  infy(z) ≤ infy(x)  and  supy(x) ≤ supy(z)  (from the chain in the y‐coordinates).  

Thus all conditions for x E z are satisfied.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. Excluding other directions
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All other directions (N, NE, SE, S, W, NW, SW, B) would require some contradiction with the fact that z lies strictly to the left of y, which lies strictly to the left of x (so x cannot lie inside or to the north/south/west of z, etc.). Checking them systematically quickly shows they fail one or more bounding‐box constraints.

Consequently, the only consistent possibility when “x E y” and “y E z” is that “x E z.”  

Answer:  E
