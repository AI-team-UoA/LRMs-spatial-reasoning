PROMPT: Let W and NW be cardinal direction relations. If region x is W of region y and region y is NW of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

From the given definitions, we know:

1)  x W y  means  
   • supx(x) ≤ infx(y),  
   • infy(y) ≤ infy(x),  
   • supy(x) ≤ supy(y).  

2)  y NW z  means  
   • supx(y) ≤ infx(z),  
   • supy(z) ≤ infy(y).  

We want to combine these to see what must hold for x relative to z.  

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HORIZONTAL (x)‐DIRECTION  
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
From x W y we have supx(x) ≤ infx(y), and from y NW z we have supx(y) ≤ infx(z).  Since for any region y (homeomorphic to a disk) one has infx(y) ≤ supx(y), we can chain  
   supx(x) ≤ infx(y) ≤ supx(y) ≤ infx(z).  
Hence,  
   supx(x) ≤ infx(z).  

That is exactly the “left‐of” condition (x lies completely to the left of z’s bounding box).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERTICAL (y)‐DIRECTION  
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
From x W y we have infy(y) ≤ infy(x) ≤ supy(x) ≤ supy(y).  
From y NW z we have supy(z) ≤ infy(y).  

Chain supy(z) ≤ infy(y) with infy(y) ≤ infy(x), giving  
   supy(z) ≤ infy(x).  
That says the top of z lies at or below the bottom of x.  In other words, x is above z.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMBINED DIRECTION FOR x RELATIVE TO z
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Putting these together:

• supx(x) ≤ infx(z)  (x is to the left of z)  
• supy(z) ≤ infy(x)  (x is above z)

By definition, a NW b ⇔ supx(a) ≤ infx(b) and supy(b) ≤ infy(a).  Substituting a = x, b = z gives exactly

   supx(x) ≤ infx(z) and supy(z) ≤ infy(x).

Hence the only consistent relation is

   x NW z.

So, if x is W of y and y is NW of z, then x must be NW of z.
