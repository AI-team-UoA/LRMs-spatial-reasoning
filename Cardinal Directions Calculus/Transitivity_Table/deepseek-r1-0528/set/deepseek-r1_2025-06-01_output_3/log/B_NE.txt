PROMPT: Let B and NE be cardinal direction relations. If region x is B of region y and region y is NE of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

Based on the given cardinal direction relations:

- \(x \text{ B } y\) implies that region \(x\) is entirely within the bounding box of region \(y\):
  - \(\inf_x(y) \leq \inf_x(x)\)
  - \(\sup_x(x) \leq \sup_x(y)\)
  - \(\inf_y(y) \leq \inf_y(x)\)
  - \(\sup_y(x) \leq \sup_y(y)\)

- \(y \text{ NE } z\) implies that region \(y\) is northeast of region \(z\):
  - \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y)\) (entire \(z\) is west of \(y\))
  - \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(y)\) (entire \(z\) is south of \(y\))

Combining these:

- From \(y \text{ NE } z\): \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y)\) and \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(y)\).
- From \(x \text{ B } y\): \(\inf_x(y) \leq \inf_x(x)\) and \(\inf_y(y) \leq \inf_y(x)\).

Thus:
- \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y) \leq \inf_x(x)\) → \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\)
- \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(y) \leq \inf_y(x)\) → \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(x)\)

These conditions match the definition of \(x \text{ NE } z\):
- \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\) (entire \(z\) is west of \(x\))
- \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(x)\) (entire \(z\) is south of \(x\))

No other cardinal direction relation (N, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW, B) is satisfied under these constraints, as they require additional spatial relationships that are not guaranteed. For example:
- \(x \text{ E } z\) would require \(\inf_y(z) \leq \inf_y(x)\) and \(\sup_y(x) \leq \sup_y(z)\), but \(\sup_y(z) \leq \inf_y(x)\) implies \(\sup_y(x) \leq \sup_y(z)\) is impossible if \(y\)-coordinates of \(x\) are above \(z\).
- Similarly, other relations fail due to mismatched horizontal or vertical bounds.

Thus, the only possible relation between \(x\) and \(z\) is **NE**.

**Answer: NE**
