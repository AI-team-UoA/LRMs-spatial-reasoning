  - \(\inf_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y)\) and \(\sup_x(y) \leq \sup_x(z)\) (y is horizontally within z's bounding box).

- Vertically: \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(y) \leq \sup_y(y) \leq \inf_y(z)\), so \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\).

Thus, x satisfies the conditions for being S of z:
- \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\),
- \(\inf_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\),
- \(\sup_x(x) \leq \sup_x(z)\).

No other cardinal direction relation (N, NE, SE, E, NW, W, SW, or B) is possible because:
- Vertically, x must be below z (\(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\)), ruling out N, NE, NW, E, W, and B (which requires vertical containment not satisfied).
