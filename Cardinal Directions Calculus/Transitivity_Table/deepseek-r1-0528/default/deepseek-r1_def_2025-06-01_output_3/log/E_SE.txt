PROMPT: Let E and SE be cardinal direction relations. If region x is E of region y and region y is SE of region z, then which could the possible relations between region x and region z be?

ANSWER:

Given that region \(x\) is E of region \(y\) and region \(y\) is SE of region \(z\), the possible relation between region \(x\) and region \(z\) is SE (South East).

### Derivation:
- From \(y\) SE \(z\):
  - \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y)\) (i.e., \(z\) is to the west of \(y\))
  - \(\sup_y(y) \leq \inf_y(z)\) (i.e., \(y\) is to the south of \(z\))

- From \(x\) E \(y\):
  - \(\sup_x(y) \leq \inf_x(x)\) (i.e., \(y\) is to the west of \(x\))
  - \(\inf_y(y) \leq \inf_y(x)\)
  - \(\sup_y(x) \leq \sup_y(y)\)

Combining the inequalities:
- **Horizontal direction (x-axis):**  
  \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(y) \leq \sup_x(y) \leq \inf_x(x)\), so \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\). This means \(z\) is entirely to the west of \(x\).

- **Vertical direction (y-axis):**  
  \(\sup_y(x) \leq \sup_y(y) \leq \inf_y(z)\), so \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\). This means \(x\) is entirely to the south of \(z\).

The conditions \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\) and \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\) match the definition of SE:  
- \(x\) SE \(z\) if and only if \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x)\) and \(\sup_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\).

No other cardinal direction relation (N, NE, E, S, SW, W, NW, B) is possible, as they would require additional or conflicting conditions that are not necessarily satisfied:
- For example, \(x\) E \(z\) would require \(\inf_y(z) \leq \inf_y(x)\), but we have \(\inf_y(x) \leq \inf_y(z)\) (from \(\inf_y(y) \leq \inf_y(x)\) and \(\sup_y(y) \leq \inf_y(z)\)), and equality is not guaranteed. Similarly, \(x\) S \(z\) would require \(\sup_x(x) \leq \sup_x(z)\), which conflicts with \(\sup_x(z) \leq \inf_x(x) \leq \sup_x(x)\).

Thus, SE is the only possible relation.

**Answer:** SE
