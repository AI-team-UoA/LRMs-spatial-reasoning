PROMPT: If region x is E of region y and region y is S of region z, then which could be the possible relations between region x and region z?

ANSWER:

Let the minimum bounding box of a region $a$ be represented by the interval $[infx(a), supx(a)] \times [infy(a), supy(a)]$.

The given conditions are:
1.  $x$ E $y$: $supx(y) \le infx(x)$, $infy(y) \le infy(x)$, and $supy(x) \le supy(y)$.
2.  $y$ S $z$: $supy(y) \le infy(z)$, $infx(z) \le infx(y)$, and $supx(y) \le supx(z)$.

We are considering regions in REG, which are homeomorphic to the closed unit disk. This implies that for any region $a \in$ REG, its minimum bounding box is non-degenerate, meaning $infx(a) < supx(a)$ and $infy(a) < supy(a)$.

Let's derive relationships between the bounding boxes of $x$ and $z$.

From condition 1 (x E y):
(1a) $supx(y) \le infx(x)$
(1b) $infy(y) \le infy(x)$
(1c) $supy(x) \le supy(y)$

From condition 2 (y S z):
(2a) $supy(y) \le infy(z)$
(2b) $infx(z) \le infx(y)$
(2c) $supx(y) \le supx(z)$

Vertical relationship between x and z:
From (1c) and (2a): $supy(x) \le supy(y)$ and $supy(y) \le infy(z)$.
Combining these, we get $supy(x) \le infy(z)$.
This inequality means that the maximum y-coordinate of region x is less than or equal to the minimum y-coordinate of region z. This corresponds to x being vertically "South" of z (or their upper/lower boundaries aligning). This relation ($supy(a) \le infy(b)$) is a component of the S, SW, and SE relations.

Horizontal relationship between x and z:
From (2b): $infx(z) \le infx(y)$. Since regions are non-degenerate, $infx(y) < supx(y)$.
So, $infx(z) \le infx(y) < supx(y)$.
From (1a): $supx(y) \le infx(x)$.
Combining $infx(z) < supx(y)$ and $supx(y) \le infx(x)$, we get $infx(z) < infx(x)$.
This means the minimum x-coordinate of z is strictly less than the minimum x-coordinate of x. Region x's left edge is always to the right of region z's left edge. This rules out x being West or North-West of z (where $supx(x) \le infx(z)$ is required, which contradicts $infx(z) < infx(x) \le supx(x)$ for non-degenerate x). It also rules out z containing x or aligning on the left (required for B).
